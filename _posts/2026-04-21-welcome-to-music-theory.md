Welcome to the maths side of music.
An octave doubles frequency: $f_2 = 2 f_1$. A perfect fifth is $3/2$. But our ears hear *ratios*, not differences — that's logarithmic perception.
In 12-TET, each semitone is:
$$ r = 2^{1/12} \approx 1.05946 $$
So a perfect fifth (7 semitones):
$$ 2^{7/12} \approx 1.4983 \approx 3/2 $$
Close enough for Bach, good enough for us.
